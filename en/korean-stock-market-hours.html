Korean Stock Market Hours

KOSPI and KOSDAQ trading hours in Korean Standard Time for overseas readers tracking Korean stocks before the U.S. or European market day begins.

Current-reference note: This page uses the Korea Exchange guide as the baseline for ordinary Korean securities-market hours. Holiday schedules, temporary first-trading-day adjustments, broker availability, and future extended-hours programs should be checked at the source before trading.

Regular Session

09:00-15:30 KST

KOSPI and KOSDAQ regular trading hours on ordinary trading days.

Pre-Hours

07:30-09:00 KST

Off-hours session listed by KRX. Available order types and broker access may differ from regular trading.

After-Hours

15:40-18:00 KST

Off-hours trading after the regular close. Liquidity and execution rules can differ from the regular session.

What Overseas Readers Should Remember

  • Korea uses Korean Standard Time, UTC+09:00.
  • Korea does not currently observe daylight saving time, so the time difference with the U.S. and Europe changes when those regions switch clocks.
  • KRX-listed stock settlement is commonly described as T+2 in the KRX trading guide.
  • January first-trading-day schedules and Korean stock market holidays can change the opening time or close the market.
  • News headlines may move during U.S. hours, but the cash equity market follows Korean market sessions unless a separate extended-hours program applies.

Frequently Asked Questions

What time does the Korean stock market open?

According to the KRX guide, the regular stock-market session is 09:00 to 15:30 Korean Standard Time on ordinary trading days.

Does Korea use daylight saving time?

Korean Standard Time is UTC+09:00 and Korea does not currently observe daylight saving time.

Are there pre-hours and after-hours sessions?

The KRX guide lists off-hours sessions, including pre-hours from 07:30 to 09:00 and after-hours trading from 15:40 to 18:00. Availability and order types can differ by product and broker.
