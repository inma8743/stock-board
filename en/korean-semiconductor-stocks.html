Semiconductor Research Checklist

  • Memory cycle: compare DRAM and NAND pricing, inventory, supply discipline, and whether earnings are moving faster than expectations.
  • HBM exposure: separate sampling, product qualification, shipment, mass production, and revenue recognition.
  • Foundry exposure: for Samsung, track advanced-node competitiveness, customer order flow, capex, and margin direction.
  • Equipment exposure: for supplier themes, verify orders, backlog, delivery timing, customer concentration, and realized revenue.
  • ETF route: if using EWY or FLKR, check current weights in Samsung Electronics, SK Hynix, and related Korean semiconductor names.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which Korean semiconductor stocks do overseas readers usually research first?

Samsung Electronics, SK Hynix, and Hanmi Semiconductor are common starting points, but each has different exposure to memory, HBM, foundry, mobile, and equipment cycles.

Are Korean semiconductor stocks only AI stocks?

No. AI demand and HBM matter, but Korean semiconductor stocks can also move with DRAM, NAND, foundry, mobile demand, equipment orders, currency, and foreign investor flows.

Can ETFs provide Korean semiconductor exposure?

Korea-focused ETFs may hold major Korean semiconductor names, but holdings and weights change. Check current issuer documents before assuming exposure.
