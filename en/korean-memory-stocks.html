DRAM, NAND, HBM: What To Separate

  • DRAM: watch server demand, PC/mobile demand, pricing, inventory, supply discipline, and whether high-end products are improving the margin mix.
  • NAND: watch enterprise SSD demand, mobile storage demand, layer-count transitions, China fab exposure, pricing, and whether loss-making cycles are turning.
  • HBM: watch customer qualification, generation transitions such as HBM3E to HBM4, packaging capacity, yield, supply agreements, and actual revenue contribution.
  • ETF exposure: Korea ETFs can provide indirect access, but holdings and weights change. Always check current issuer holdings before assuming Samsung or SK Hynix exposure.

Memory Cycle Checklist

  • Are DRAM and NAND average selling prices rising, falling, or only improving in premium categories?
  • Is inventory being drawn down, or are customers still digesting prior purchases?
  • Are capex plans demand-backed, or could new supply pressure future pricing?
  • Are HBM headlines already reflected in expectations, margins, and valuation?
  • Is the move driven by company fundamentals, Korea market flows, currency, or global AI risk appetite?

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the main Korean memory stocks?

Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix are the main Korean memory names overseas readers usually research first. Related equipment and packaging suppliers may also move with the memory cycle.

What is the difference between DRAM, NAND, and HBM for investors?

DRAM is closely tied to computing and server memory demand, NAND is tied to storage demand, and HBM is a high-performance DRAM category used in AI accelerators and high-performance computing.

Are Korean memory stocks the same as AI stocks?

Not exactly. AI demand can be a major driver through HBM and server memory, but Korean memory stocks also respond to broader DRAM pricing, NAND pricing, inventory, capex, mobile demand, and currency moves.
